Two Pointer Recipe

Problem

You have a string of ascii characters. You want to reverse the order of the vowels while leaving the order of the consonants the same. How can you do it?

Solution Recipe

The solution to your problem is the Two Pointer Recipe. The Two Pointer Recipe is a simple recipe that can help you solve many LeetCode problems.

We will have two pointers, one starting on the left and one starting on the right. Overall, we will do the following until the left pointer is to the right of the right pointer.

  1. Move the left pointer right to the next vowel.
  2. Move the right pointer left to the next vowel.
  3. If the left pointer is still to the left of the green arrow, we will swap the vowels to which the pointers point.

Now, a demonstration on a sample string: 'odinproject'. In the diagrams that follow, our left pointer is the red arrow and the right pointer is the green arrow. We will initialize them to point to -1 and the 14 (the length of our string), respectively. This means they point outside the string.

Following our recipe from above, we move the red arrow right to the next vowel, the 'o'.

Now, move the green arrow left to the next vowel, the 'e'.

Swap the 'o' and the 'e'.

Again, we repeat our steps. Move the red arrow right to the next vowel.

Move the green arrow left to the next vowel.

Swap the vowels.

We now repeat our steps. Move the red arrow right to the next vowel.

Move the green arrow left to the next vowel.

Now something is different. The red arrow is to the right of the green arrow. It is now time to stop; we have swapped all the vowels and have our answer.
